The Ringers

The Lockerley and East Tytherley Bell Ringers are an active band. The band has a reputation for being friendly and welcoming to visitors. We go to the pub after practice on a Thursday and go for coffee after ringing for the service on a Sunday. We also organise social events including an annual ringing outing and Christmas dinner. We are enthusiastic ringers typically ring several quarter-peals each year and some of the band also ring at other towers. Visiting bands who wish to ring at either tower should contact the Tower Secretary. Links to maps and further contact details can be found at the Lockerley Tower and East Tytherley Tower web pages.

Practices

We practice on the first, third, and fifth Thursdays of the month at St John's Church, Lockerley and on the second and fourth Thursdays of the month at St Peter's Church, East Tytherley. Practices start at 19:00 and end at 20:30. At a typical practice night we ring a variety of methods ranging from Plain Hunt to Stedman Doubles and sometimes manage to ring some Surprise Minor with the help of some of our visitors. On the eight bells at East Tytherley we also ring triples methods, typically Plain Bob or Grandsire. Visitors are always very welcome to come along to practices. We also hold additional practices from time to time using the simulator at Lockerley. Separate practices are held for learners when required.

Service Ringing

We ring for services at St John's Church, Lockerley except for the third Sunday in the month when we ring at St Peter's Church, East Tytherley (fifth Sundays in the month are variable). Service ringing starts at 10:00 for a service that starts at 10:30 (ringing starts at 10:30 for services at East Tytherley). Service ringing typically consists of a variety of doubles methods and call changes.

The Bells

St John's church in Lockerley has six bells, the tenor weighs 12 cwt, cast by John Taylor & Co. in 1890 and rehung in 2002. All the bells are easy to ring and make a nice sound. The tower has a simulator installed which is used both by the local band and the Guild for listening skills practice.

St Peter's church in East Tytherley has eight bells, the tenor weighs 14 cwt, cast by John Taylor & Co. in 1897 (the two trebles were added two years later). The bells have not been rehung and are still on original plain bearings; this, coupled with a fairly long draft, make them slightly harder to ring than the bells at Lockerley and less suitable for teaching learners, although most ringers have little difficulty ringing them. The second bell is hung above the other bells making it harder to hear above the sound of the other bells, a sound tube has just been installed to improve the sound balance in the ringing room. Work has been carried out to install sound insulation which makes the sound level in the ringing room far more comfortable than previously.

Learning to ring

Anyone who would like to try bell ringing is very welcome to have a go, the only requirement is that you are at least eight years old. We are always keen to attract new recruits to make sure we continue to have a strong band into the future. If you would like to find out more, try visiting this web page. If you would like to have a try, please contact the Tower Captain.
