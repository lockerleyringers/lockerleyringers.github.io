History of the bells at St John's Church, Lockerley

Original installation

A ring of six bells cast in 1890 by John Taylor & Co (Bellfounders) of Loughborough, replaced the two bells in the earlier church.

Five of the bells are in a cast iron “H pattern” frame carried on timber bearers; the remaining bell, the second, is in a timber sub-frame above the third. The bells and bellframe were refurbished by Haywood Mills Associates in 2002.

The bells, together with their frame and fittings, were by their inscriptions the gift of the Dalgety family. John Taylor & Co’s indication of the cost for the work including the frame being £358-12-0, less an unknown allowance for the two bells removed.

As originally installed the bells were hung from timber headstocks carried on plain brass bearings mounted in cast iron housings bolted to the “H” section frame members. The two smallest bells (treble and second) were cast with canons, or loops, on their heads and fitted to the headstocks by traditional canon straps whilst the four larger bells were cast with flat heads and fitted to the headstocks with independent bell support bolts in the modern fashion. The clappers to all the bells were of wrought iron.

Refurbishment

The 2002 refurbishment by Haywood Mills and Associates was comprehensive. The work included the tuning of the bells to the “five-tone” principle (the “Prime” note of the tenor is G, resonating at 798.5 Hz) and quarter turning to even out the wear caused by the striking of the clapper. The provision of cast iron headstocks, carried on double row, self-aligning, ball bearings. The canons were removed from the treble and second and resin was cast on to present a flat surface for fitting to the new headstocks by independent bolts. The existing cast iron frame was refurbished, the timber bearers replaced, steel bracing and stiffeners have been provided to the sub-frame for the 'second'. The existing bell wheels were adapted to suit the new headstocks. New clappers of spheroidal graphite cast iron have been fitted.

Prior to rehanging in the refurbished frame the bells were balanced in the new headstocks.
